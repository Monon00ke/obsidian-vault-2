## Key Points

### Positive impact
- **Stay connected** with friends and family across the world
- **Learn new things** — educational content, news, tutorials
- **Career opportunities** — networking on LinkedIn, SMM jobs
- **Self-expression** — share creativity, start a blog
- **Community building** — find people with similar interests
- **Business** — promote products, reach customers

### Negative impact
- **Addiction** — endless scrolling wastes time
- **Mental health** — comparison, anxiety, depression
- **Privacy** — data collection, tracking
- **Cyberbullying** — online harassment
- **Misinformation** — fake news spreads fast
- **Real-life relationships suffer** — less face-to-face communication

### SMM (Social Media Marketing)
- Companies use social media to promote products
- Influencers earn money through sponsored posts
- Targeted ads based on user data
- Engagement metrics: likes, shares, comments

## Sample Answer — Part 1

> **Examiner:** How much time do you spend on social media?

> **To be honest**, I spend quite a lot of time on social media — maybe 2 or 3 hours a day. I mainly use Instagram and Telegram to **keep in touch** with friends and follow news. **However**, I've noticed that sometimes I just **doom-scroll** without any purpose, so I'm trying to reduce my **screen time**.

## Sample Answer — Part 2 (Describe your favourite social media platform)

> **I'd like to talk about** Instagram, which is probably the platform I use most often. **The first thing that comes to mind** is that it's incredibly visual and easy to use.

> I follow various **content creators** — photographers, chefs, and travel bloggers. **What strikes me the most** is how Instagram's **algorithm** always shows me content that I'm genuinely interested in. **Moreover**, I've learned a lot through short educational reels.

> **On the downside**, sometimes I catch myself **comparing** my life to the perfect pictures people post, and that can be unhealthy. **All in all**, I think Instagram is a great tool if you use it mindfully.

## Sample Answer — Part 3

> **Examiner:** Do you think social media has improved communication?

> **That's a good question.** **In my opinion**, social media has both improved and damaged communication. **On the one hand**, it's now incredibly easy to **stay in touch** with people across the world. **Moreover**, introverts can express themselves more easily online.

> **On the other hand**, I'd argue that online communication is quite **superficial**. People send emojis instead of having real conversations. **Generally speaking**, we have more connections but fewer **meaningful relationships**.

> **That being said**, it also depends on how you use it. Some people build deep communities online. **At the end of the day**, social media is just a tool — it's how you use it that matters.

## IELTS Writing Examples

### Essay 1 — Social media: connecting or isolating?

**Prompt:** Some people think social media brings people together, while others believe it isolates them. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

> Social media has become an integral part of modern life. While some argue that it strengthens human connections, I believe that, despite its benefits, it can also lead to isolation.

> **On the one hand**, social media allows people to **stay in touch** with friends and family across the globe. **To give you an example**, during the pandemic, many people relied on platforms like Zoom and WhatsApp to maintain relationships. **Moreover**, social media helps build communities around shared interests — from book clubs to fitness groups. It also provides a platform for marginalized voices to be heard.

> **On the other hand**, critics argue that online interactions are **superficial** compared to face-to-face communication. People may have hundreds of **followers** but very few real friends. **Furthermore**, excessive **screen time** can lead to addiction, anxiety, and **FOMO** (fear of missing out). **In my opinion**, the quality of social interaction has declined as people spend more time **doom-scrolling** through their **news feed** instead of having real conversations.

> **In conclusion**, social media is a double-edged sword. It can connect us, but it can also isolate us. **At the end of the day**, it depends on how we use it — mindfully or mindlessly.

### Letter 1 — Formal: Complaint about data privacy

> Dear Customer Support,

> I am writing to express my concern regarding recent changes to your platform's **privacy policy**. I have been an active user for several years, but I am worried about how my personal data is being collected and used.

> **To be specific**, I have noticed an increase in **targeted ads** that appear to be based on private conversations I had through your messaging service. **I would like to clarify** whether my messages are being monitored for advertising purposes.

> **I would appreciate it if you could** provide a clear explanation of your data collection practices. **Should you require any further information**, please contact me at the email address above.

> I look forward to your prompt response.

> Yours faithfully,

> [Your Name]

### Letter 2 — Informal: Giving advice about social media detox

> Hey Mike,

> Thanks for your message! I'm glad you're thinking about a **digital detox** — honestly, I think it's a great idea.

> **In my opinion**, you should start small. Maybe try **no social media for the first hour** after waking up. That's what I did, and it made a huge difference. **To give you an example**, I now read a book instead of scrolling through Instagram in the morning.

> **As for** apps to keep, I'd suggest keeping Telegram for messaging your family, but deleting Instagram and TikTok for a while. You'll be surprised how much free time you have!

> Let me know how it goes!

> Best,

> Stepan
