## Key Points

### Почему люди любят шопинг

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Dopamine hit** | Buying triggers pleasure in the brain |
| **Self-expression** | What you buy reflects your personality |
| **Retail therapy** | Shopping can improve mood temporarily |
| **Social activity** | Shopping with friends is fun |
| **Need** | Buying essential items for survival |

### История шопинга
- **Past**: People only bought what they needed — food, clothes, tools
- **Present**: Shopping became entertainment — malls, online stores 24/7
- **Future**: More online shopping, personalised ads, AI recommendations

## Sample Answer — Part 1

> **Examiner:** Do you like shopping?

> **To be honest**, it depends on what I'm shopping for. **Generally speaking**, I don't enjoy shopping for clothes — I find it tiring. But I love shopping for electronics or books. **For example**, I can spend hours browsing gadgets online.

> **However**, I try not to be an **impulse buyer**. I usually make a list and stick to my **budget**. **In my opinion**, it's important to find **value for money** rather than buying things just because they're **on sale**.

## Sample Answer — Part 2 (Describe a shopping experience)

> **The first thing that comes to mind** is when I bought my first laptop with my own money. **I can still remember** the feeling of walking into the store and knowing I could **afford** it after months of saving.

> I had done a lot of research beforehand — read reviews, compared prices, and checked specifications. **What strikes me the most** is the sense of accomplishment I felt when I finally made the purchase. It wasn't just a laptop — it was the result of hard work and discipline.

> **Moreover**, I got a great **bargain** because it was **on sale** during Black Friday. **All in all**, it was a positive experience because I had planned it well and felt no **buyer's remorse**.

## Sample Answer — Part 3

> **Examiner:** Is shopping becoming too important in modern society?

> **Without a doubt**, I think shopping has become a major part of modern life. **In my opinion**, we live in a **consumer society** where people often define themselves by what they own.

> **On the one hand**, shopping is good for the economy — it creates jobs and drives innovation. **Moreover**, it can be a source of pleasure and **retail therapy**.

> **On the other hand**, I believe that excessive **consumerism** is a problem. People buy things they don't need to impress people they don't like. **At the end of the day**, material possessions shouldn't be the main source of happiness.

> **To put it bluntly**, we need to shift from quantity to quality — buy less but better. **That's my take on it.**

## IELTS Writing Examples

### Essay 1 — Shopping as entertainment

**Prompt:** Many people see shopping as a form of entertainment rather than a necessity. Is this a positive or negative trend?

> In modern society, shopping has evolved from a basic necessity into a popular leisure activity. While this has some benefits, I believe the negative consequences are more significant.

> **On the one hand**, shopping can be a legitimate form of entertainment. **Retail therapy** can improve mood temporarily, and shopping with friends is a social activity. **Moreover**, the shopping experience itself has become more enjoyable — modern malls offer restaurants, cinemas, and entertainment zones. **In my opinion**, there is nothing wrong with occasionally treating yourself.

> **On the other hand**, treating shopping as entertainment encourages **consumerism** and materialism. People buy things they do not need just for the pleasure of buying, which leads to **overspending** and debt. **Furthermore**, this behaviour is harmful to the environment because it increases waste and resource consumption.

> **In conclusion**, while shopping can be fun, it should not be our primary form of entertainment. **At the end of the day**, there are more fulfilling and sustainable ways to spend our free time — like hobbies, sports, or spending time in nature.

### Letter 2 — Informal: Describing a shopping spree

> Hey Dima!

> You won't believe what happened — I went shopping yesterday and totally went overboard!

> I found this amazing sale at a electronics store — everything was **up to 50% off**! **To be honest**, I didn't really need a new smartwatch, but it was such a **bargain** that I couldn't resist. **I also picked up** a couple of games for my console.

> **The best part** was that the cashier gave me an extra **discount code** for my next purchase. **All in all**, I came home with way more than I planned, but I don't regret it!

> Let me know if you want to check out the sale — it's on until Sunday!

> Cheers,
> Stepan
