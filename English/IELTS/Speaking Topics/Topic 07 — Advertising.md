## Key Points

### Зачем нужна реклама
- **Inform** consumers about new products
- **Persuade** people to buy
- **Build brand image** and recognition
- **Support free content** — YouTube, social media are free because of ads

### Нравится ли нам реклама?
| Pro (likes) | Con (dislikes) |
|-------------|----------------|
| Useful for finding new products | Too intrusive, interrupts experience |
| Some ads are creative and funny | Pop-ups are annoying |
| Targeted ads can be relevant | Privacy concerns |
| Supports free services | Misleading claims |
| Helps compare options | Creates artificial needs |

### Влияние на разные аудитории
| Audience | How Ads Target Them |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Children** | Colourful, fun, cartoon characters |
| **Teenagers** | Influencers, social media, trends |
| **Adults** | Utility, price, quality, reviews |
| **Elderly** | Health, comfort, family values |

### Таргетированная реклама
- Uses your browsing history, likes, location
- Algorithms predict what you'll buy
- Can be creepy but effective
- Ethical concerns about privacy

## Sample Answer — Part 1

> **Examiner:** Do you like advertisements?

> **Well, I'd say** it depends on the type of advertisement. **Generally speaking**, I don't mind ads that are creative and subtle — like a funny commercial or a well-made billboard. But **I'm not so sure about** intrusive ads, like pop-up ads or spam emails. **To be honest**, I use an ad-blocker when I browse the internet because some ads are just too annoying.

## Sample Answer — Part 2 (Describe an advertisement you remember)

> **The first thing that comes to mind** is a Nike commercial I saw last year. **What strikes me the most** is that it wasn't just about shoes — it was about motivation and overcoming challenges.

> The ad showed athletes training in the rain, getting up after falling, and pushing their limits. The slogan was: "Just do it." **To give you an example**, there was a scene with a young runner who kept falling and getting back up — it was really inspiring.

> **In my opinion**, the best advertisements don't just sell products — they tell a story. **Moreover**, this ad used emotional appeal rather than listing technical specifications. **All in all**, it was memorable because it made me feel something.

## Sample Answer — Part 3

> **Examiner:** Do you think advertising influences children too much?

> **That's a good question.** **Without a doubt**, children are very vulnerable to advertising. They see a colourful toy commercial and immediately want it. **To my mind**, companies that target children should be more responsible.

> **In my opinion**, some advertising aimed at children is **misleading** — it makes products seem more exciting than they really are. **Moreover**, children don't understand the persuasive intent of ads.

> **However**, I think parents also have a role to play. They should teach their children to be critical about advertisements. **At the end of the day**, advertising is everywhere — we just need to be smart about it.

## IELTS Writing Examples

### Essay 1 — Advertising manipulates consumers

**Prompt:** Advertising often manipulates people into buying things they do not need. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

> I strongly agree that advertising manipulates consumers, although I believe it also serves some useful purposes.

> **On the one hand**, advertising uses sophisticated psychological techniques to influence our behaviour. **To give you an example**, ads create a sense of urgency with phrases like "limited offer" or "only 3 left" — this is the **scarcity** technique. **Moreover**, **celebrity endorsements** make us trust products simply because a famous person uses them. Children are particularly vulnerable to **emotional appeals** and colourful commercials.

> **Furthermore**, **targeted advertising** based on our browsing history invades our privacy and makes it harder to resist impulse buying. **As a result**, many people accumulate debt buying things they never needed in the first place.

> **On the other hand**, advertising also provides useful information. It helps consumers discover new products and compare options. **Without advertising**, many free online services like YouTube and social media would not exist. **In my opinion**, the problem is not advertising itself, but manipulative and misleading practices.

> **In conclusion**, while advertising can be useful, it is clear that it often manipulates consumers. **At the end of the day**, the best defence is critical thinking and media literacy.

### Essay 2 — Banning advertising aimed at children

**Prompt:** Some people believe that advertising aimed at children should be banned. Do you agree or disagree?

> I completely agree that advertising targeting children should be banned or heavily restricted, as children are not capable of understanding the persuasive intent of commercials.

> **First of all**, young children cannot distinguish between entertainment and advertising. When they see a colourful **commercial** for a sugary cereal or a toy, they believe it is true and pressure their parents to buy it. **This is unfair** both to children, who may develop unhealthy habits, and to parents, who face constant nagging.

> **Furthermore**, advertising to children often promotes materialistic values — the idea that happiness comes from owning things. **In my opinion**, this is harmful to a child's development.

> **However**, some argue that parents should simply say "no" and teach their children about advertising. **While I agree** that parental guidance is important, banning ads aimed at children would provide a necessary layer of protection.

> **In conclusion**, advertising to children is manipulative and should be banned. **Without a doubt**, protecting children's wellbeing is more important than corporate profits.

### Letter — Formal: Complaint about misleading advertisement

> Dear Customer Service,

> I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with a product I purchased after seeing your advertisement on social media.

> **The advertisement claimed** that your wireless headphones had 40 hours of battery life and noise cancellation. However, in reality, the battery lasts only about 10 hours and the noise cancellation is virtually non-existent. **To put it bluntly**, the product does **not match the description** in the ad.

> **I would like to request** a full refund or a replacement that matches the advertised specifications. **I look forward to** your prompt response regarding this matter.

> Yours faithfully,
> [Your Name]
