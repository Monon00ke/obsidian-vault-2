## Key Points

### Что такое сплетни и почему люди сплетничают

| Reason | Explanation | Translation |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Social bonding** | Sharing gossip creates a sense of connection | Социальная связь |
| **Boredom** | People gossip when they have nothing else to do | От скуки |
| **Attention** | Being the one who knows things makes you interesting | Желание внимания |
| **Social comparison** | Comparing yourself to others makes you feel better | Сравнение с другими |
| **Group acceptance** | Gossiping can be a way to fit in | Принятие в группе |
| **Competition** | Spreading negative info can hurt rivals | Конкуренция |
| **Entertainment** | Gossip can be fun and dramatic | Развлечение |

### Кто сплетничает

| Group | What they gossip about |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Relatives** | Family news, marriages, divorces |
| **Colleagues** | Who got promoted, salary, office romance |
| **Neighbours** | Who moved in, who is renovating |
| **Friends** | Relationships, personal problems |
| **Classmates** | Grades, who is dating whom |

### Psychological perspective
- Humans are social animals — we need to know about others
- Gossip releases oxytocin (bonding hormone)
- It's a form of social learning — learning from others' mistakes
- Evolutionary: knowing who to trust and who to avoid

## Sample Answer — Part 1

> **Examiner:** Do you like to gossip?

> **To be honest**, I think everyone gossips to some extent — even if they don't call it that. **Generally speaking**, I don't like **malicious gossip** that can hurt someone's reputation. But **harmless gossip** — like talking about what a mutual friend is doing — is a normal part of social life. **In my opinion**, it becomes a problem when people **exaggerate** or **distort the truth**.

## Sample Answer — Part 2 (Describe a situation when you heard some gossip)

> **The first thing that comes to mind** is a situation at my university. Someone started a **rumour** that one of our professors was leaving for another job. **It spread like wildfire** through the **grapevine** — everyone was talking about it.

> **What strikes me the most** is how quickly people believed it without checking the facts. Some students even said they had "heard it from a reliable source." Later, it turned out to be completely false — the professor wasn't going anywhere.

> **In my opinion**, this shows how dangerous gossip can be. **Moreover**, even when the truth came out, the damage was already done — some people still had doubts. **All in all**, it was a good lesson in not believing everything you hear.

## Sample Answer — Part 3

> **Examiner:** Is gossip always a bad thing?

> **That's an interesting question.** **To my mind**, gossip isn't always negative. **On the one hand**, **malicious gossip** can destroy reputations, cause **trust issues**, and damage relationships. **To give you an example**, workplace gossip can create a toxic environment where no one feels safe.

> **On the other hand**, some researchers argue that gossip is a natural **social bonding** mechanism. It helps people understand social norms and learn from others' experiences. **Moreover**, talking about others can be a way of sharing information that helps the group function better.

> **Without a doubt**, the key difference is intention. If you're gossiping to hurt someone — that's wrong. But if you're simply sharing information or expressing concern, it's a normal human behaviour. **That's my take on it.**

## IELTS Writing Examples

### Essay 1 — Gossip: harmless or harmful?

**Prompt:** Some people think gossip is just harmless social talk, while others believe it causes serious damage. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

> Gossip is a universal human behaviour, but opinions differ on whether it is harmless or harmful. In my view, it depends on the intention behind it.

> **On the one hand**, gossip can serve important social functions. It helps people **bond** and establish trust within a group. **To give you an example**, sharing information about a colleague's promotion or a friend's wedding is a way of showing interest in others' lives. **Moreover**, gossip can act as a warning system — if you hear that someone has been dishonest, you can protect yourself.

> **On the other hand**, **malicious gossip** can destroy reputations and relationships. **Spreading rumours** about someone behind their back can lead to **trust issues**, anxiety, and even depression. **In the workplace**, gossip can create a toxic environment where no one feels safe. **Furthermore**, once a **rumour** is out, it is very difficult to correct the damage, even if the truth comes out later.

> **In my opinion**, the key factor is intention. **Harmless gossip** is part of social interaction, but when it becomes **malicious** or is used to manipulate, it is clearly harmful. **At the end of the day**, we should all try to be more mindful of what we say about others.

### Letter — Informal: Sharing some harmless gossip with a friend

> Dear Katya,

> I hope you're doing well! I have some **juicy gossip** for you — you won't believe what happened!

> So, remember our old classmate Misha? Apparently, he quit his job and is moving to Bali to become a yoga instructor! I heard it **through the grapevine** from Anna, who saw his Instagram post.

> **To be honest**, I'm not sure if it's true — you know how **rumours** can get **exaggerated**. **But if it is true**, good for him! He always hated his office job.

> Anyway, that's the news from here. Let me know if you've heard anything else!

> Lots of love,
> Stepan
