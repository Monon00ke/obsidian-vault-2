## Key Points

### Что такое осознанное потребление?

Conscious consumption is about thinking before you buy:
- **Do I really need this?**
- **Can I afford it?**
- **Will it last?**
- **How was it made?**
- **What happens when I throw it away?**

### Вопросы, которые нужно себе задавать

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| **Do I need it or just want it?** | Separates necessity from impulse |
| **Will I use it at least 30 times?** | Avoids clothes that sit in the wardrobe |
| **Can I borrow or rent it?** | Reduces unnecessary ownership |
| **Is it good quality?** | Saves money long-term |
| **Is it made ethically?** | Supports fair labour practices |
| **Is there an eco-friendly alternative?** | Reduces environmental harm |
| **What will happen to it after I'm done?** | Considers recyclability |

### Зачем нужно осознанное потребление

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Environmental protection** | Less waste, less pollution, less resource depletion |
| **Economic stability** | Overconsumption leads to economic bubbles and waste |
| **Personal finance** | Saves money, reduces debt |
| **Mental health** | Less clutter, less stress, more gratitude |
| **Social justice** | Avoids supporting unethical labour practices |

### Как consumption влияет на экономику

| Concept | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Supply & demand** | High demand → more production → more emissions |
| **The more you consume** | The more resources are extracted |
| **Which leads to** | More waste, pollution, CO2 emissions |
| **Which needs to be stabilised** | To create a sustainable economy |

## Sample Answer — Part 1

> **Examiner:** Do you think you're a conscious consumer?

> **To be honest**, I try to be, but I'm not perfect. **Generally speaking**, I always ask myself if I really need something before buying it. **In my opinion**, it's important to focus on **quality over quantity**.

> **However**, I sometimes make **impulse purchases**, especially when there's a sale. **That being said**, I have started buying more **second-hand** clothes and I always carry a reusable bag. **All in all**, I think every small step helps.

## Sample Answer — Part 3

> **Examiner:** Is overconsumption a serious problem today?

> **Without a doubt**, I think overconsumption is one of the biggest challenges of our time. **More often than not**, people buy things they don't need, and this creates enormous waste.

> **In my opinion**, there are several reasons for this. First, advertising constantly tells us we need new things. Second, our **throwaway culture** makes it easy to replace rather than repair. And third, social media creates FOMO — we want what influencers have.

> **What strikes me the most** is the environmental impact. The more we consume, the more factories produce, and the more emissions are released. **At the end of the day**, overconsumption is not just a personal issue — it's a global problem.

> **However, I believe** the solution starts with individuals. If more people practice **mindful shopping**, companies will have to adapt. **That's my take on it.**

## IELTS Writing Examples

### Essay 1 — Overconsumption and the environment

**Prompt:** Overconsumption of goods is damaging the environment. What are the causes of this problem and what solutions can you suggest?

> In recent decades, overconsumption has become a serious environmental issue. This essay will analyse the main causes of this problem and propose possible solutions.

> **The primary cause** is the rise of **consumerism** and materialistic values. Advertising constantly encourages people to buy more, and **fast fashion** makes clothes so cheap that people treat them as disposable. **Moreover**, **planned obsolescence** — designing products to break quickly — forces consumers to replace items more often than necessary.

> **Another cause** is the convenience of modern shopping. With **e-commerce** and **one-click ordering**, buying things has never been easier. **To give you an example**, you can order a product from another continent and have it delivered to your door within days, generating enormous **carbon emissions** from transport and packaging.

> **To address this problem**, governments could introduce taxes on **single-use plastics** and non-essential goods. **Furthermore**, companies should be required to design products that last longer and can be repaired. **At an individual level**, people should practice **mindful shopping** — asking themselves whether they truly need an item before buying it.

> **In conclusion**, overconsumption is a complex problem driven by advertising, convenience, and planned obsolescence. **At the end of the day**, both governments and individuals must take responsibility for building a more sustainable future.

### Letter — Formal: Suggesting sustainable practices to a company

> Dear Sir/Madam,

> I am writing to suggest ways your company could adopt more sustainable practices.

> **As a loyal customer**, I appreciate your products, but I am concerned about the amount of plastic packaging used. **I would like to suggest** switching to biodegradable or recycled materials.

> **Furthermore**, offering a discount to customers who bring their own bags would encourage **conscious consumption** and improve your brand image. **In my opinion**, these small changes would make a significant difference.

> **I hope you will consider** these suggestions. Thank you for your attention.

> Yours faithfully,
> [Your Name]
