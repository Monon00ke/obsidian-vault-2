## Key Points

### Основные причины сплетен

| Cause | Explanation | Translation |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **To make friends** | Sharing info helps bond with others | Поиск друзей |
| **To gain social status** | Knowing things makes you important | Повышение статуса |
| **Group acceptance** | Gossiping shows you're part of the group | Принятие группой |
| **Socialisation** | It fills awkward silences | Социализация |
| **Dissatisfaction with life** | Makes your own life seem better by comparison | Недовольство своей жизнью |
| **Attention** | People listen to you when you have news | Желание внимания |
| **Competition / compromise** | Gossip can weaken rivals | Компромат |
| **Boredom** | Nothing better to talk about | Скука |
| **Revenge** | Getting back at someone | Месть |
| **Entertainment** | Drama is exciting | Развлечение |

### Почему мы сплетничаем — психология

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Evolutionary** | Our ancestors needed to know who was trustworthy in the tribe |
| **Social learning** | We learn from others' mistakes without making them ourselves |
| **Catharsis** | Talking about frustrations relieves stress |
| **Bonding** | Shared secrets create intimacy |
| **Comparison** | Gossiping about others boosts our own self-esteem |

### Как сплетни связаны с социальной иерархией

| Level | How Gossip Functions |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Top of hierarchy** | Gossip flows through them, they control information |
| **Middle** | Gossip as a tool to climb the ladder |
| **Bottom** | Gossip as a way to feel included |
| **Outside the group** | Gossip about them reinforces group boundaries |

## Sample Answer — Part 1

> **Examiner:** Why do people gossip?

> **Generally speaking**, I think gossip is a very natural human behaviour. **From my perspective**, people gossip for several reasons. First, it's a form of **social bonding** — sharing information about others makes you feel closer to the person you're talking to. **Moreover**, some people gossip because of **boredom** or **insecurity**.

> **To give you an example**, in school, students often gossip because they want to fit in or be part of a group. **At the end of the day**, it's often about **attention** or **social status**.

## Sample Answer — Part 3

> **Examiner:** What makes someone a gossipmonger?

> **That's a good question.** **In my opinion**, people who gossip a lot often have **low self-esteem** or are **dissatisfied with their own lives**. By talking about others, they feel better about themselves — it's a form of **social comparison**.

> **Moreover**, some people are simply **attention-seeking**. They know that **juicy gossip** makes people listen to them, which gives them a sense of importance. **To put it another way**, gossip is a way to get **social currency**.

> **However**, I think we should be careful not to judge too harshly. **At the end of the day**, most people gossip occasionally — it's part of **human nature**. The problem is when gossip becomes **malicious** or is used to deliberately hurt someone.

## Full Discussion — Can we stop gossiping?

### Arguments for "Yes, we should try to stop"
- It causes **real harm** — people lose jobs, relationships end
- It creates a **toxic environment** at work or school
- **Trust** is damaged when people know you talk behind their backs
- It's ultimately **unproductive** and a **waste of time**

### Arguments for "No, it's impossible"
- It's **innate behaviour** — even animals show similar behaviour
- Information sharing is **evolutionarily beneficial**
- Trying to ban gossip makes it more exciting (**forbidden fruit** effect)
- Some gossip is actually **positive** — sharing warnings, celebrating others

### Balanced conclusion
> **That being said**, I think the goal shouldn't be to completely eliminate gossip, but to be **mindful** of it. Ask yourself: is this gossip **harmless** or **malicious**? Would I say this if the person were here? **If I were in that situation**, I would want people to talk to me directly instead of behind my back.

## IELTS Writing Examples

### Essay 1 — Why do people gossip?

**Prompt:** Gossip is a widespread phenomenon. Why do people engage in it, and is it always a bad thing?

> Gossip is an almost universal human behaviour, occurring in every culture and society. In my opinion, people gossip for several deep-seated psychological and social reasons, and it is not always negative.

> **First of all**, gossip serves as a tool for **social bonding**. When people share information about others, they create a sense of intimacy and trust. **To put it another way**, sharing a secret makes people feel closer to each other. **Moreover**, gossip helps maintain **social norms** — by talking about someone who broke the rules, we reinforce what is acceptable behaviour.

> **Another reason** for gossip is **insecurity**. People who are **dissatisfied with their own lives** may gossip to make themselves feel better through **social comparison**. **To give you an example**, criticising a colleague's mistake might make someone feel more competent. **Furthermore**, gossip can be a form of **attention-seeking** — being the one who knows things makes you interesting and important in the group.

> **In my opinion**, gossip becomes harmful when it is done with **malicious intent** — to hurt someone's reputation or to manipulate others. **However**, mild gossip can be a normal and even healthy part of social interaction. **At the end of the day**, it is not gossip itself that is the problem, but the intention behind it.
