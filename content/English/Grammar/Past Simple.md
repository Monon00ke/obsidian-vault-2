# Past Simple

## Formula

| | Structure | Example |
|---|-----------|---------|
| **Affirmative** | Subject + V2 (regular: -ed) | I **played** football. |
| **Negative** | Subject + did not + V1 | She **did not** **go** to school. |
| **Question** | Did + Subject + V1? | **Did** you **see** him? |

## When to Use

| Use Case | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Completed action in the past** (завершённое действие) | I **visited** Paris last year. |
| **Sequence of past events** (последовательность) | He **entered** the room, **sat** down, and **started** working. |
| **Past facts & states** (факты прошлого) | She **lived** in London in 2010. |
| **Past habits** (привычки в прошлом) | When I was a child, I **played** outside every day. |

## Regular Verbs — Pronunciation of -ed

| Sound | Rule | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| /t/ | after voiceless sounds (p, k, f, s, sh, ch) | worked, laughed, watched |
| /d/ | after voiced sounds (b, g, l, m, n, r, v, z) | played, lived, called |
| /ɪd/ | after d, t | wanted, needed, decided |

## Examples

- I **walked** to work yesterday.
- We **didn't watch** TV last night.
- **Did** you **enjoy** the party?
- She **was** born in 2001.
- They **went** to Italy and **visited** Rome.

## Signal Words

yesterday, last night/week/month/year, ago (two days ago), in 2015, when, then, just now, once, one day, the day before yesterday

## Past Simple vs Present Perfect

| Past Simple | Present Perfect |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Finished time** — I **saw** him **yesterday**. | **Unfinished time** — I **have seen** him **today**. |
| **Details matter** — She **bought** it for $10. | **Result matters** — She **has bought** a car. |
| **No connection to now** — He **lived** in Paris. | **Connection to now** — He **has lived** in Paris for 10 years. |

## Spelling Rules for Regular Verbs

| Rule | Base | Past Simple |
|------|------|-------------|
| Most verbs: +ed | work → **worked**, play → **played** |
| -e: +d | live → **lived**, love → **loved** |
| Consonant + y: -y + ied | study → **studied**, try → **tried** |
| CVC* double final consonant | stop → **stopped**, plan → **planned** |
| *CVC = consonant-vowel-consonant stressed syllable |
